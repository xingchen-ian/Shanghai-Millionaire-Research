A Game Born in War

In the late 1930s, as Europe descended into chaos and refugees flooded into Shanghai's International Settlement, someone printed an English-language board game called The Game of Shanghai Millionaire — a pirated copy of Waddington's London edition of Monopoly, with Shanghai streets replacing London ones.

The manufacturer remains unknown. This project traces the game's history, its contexts, and the candidates who may have printed it.
