Research Track 04

Resources

Archives, databases, maps, and key references for Shanghai Millionaire research.

Archives & Databases

EHRI Portal

Lobel brothers' Shanghai Millionaire — catalog entry with collection details and Holocaust-era context.

portal.ehri-project.eu

World of Monopoly — China

Albert C. Veldhuis's detailed documentation of the commercial Shanghai Millionaire edition, with physical description and provenance.

worldofmonopoly.com

Rickshaw.org — From Marbles and Packs

Ralph Harpuder's personal recollection of playing Shanghai Millionaire as a child in Shanghai. Identifies Wing On as a retail point.

rickshaw.org

JDC Archives — Refuge in Shanghai

American Jewish Joint Distribution Committee's records on Shanghai refugee operations, 1938–1953. Includes photographs and organizational records.

archives.jdc.org

Virtual Shanghai — Maps

Christian Henriot's digital humanities project with historical maps of Shanghai, including a 1935–1940 printing press distribution map (ID: 876).

virtualshanghai.net

Shanghai Hong List 1880–1941

Complete business directory of Shanghai (Excel, 2.6MB). Searchable by "print"/"printing"/"press" to identify all registered printing companies. CC-BY 4.0.

zenodo.org

US Holocaust Memorial Museum

Houses the Lobel brothers' handmade Shanghai Millionaire (Accession 2009.106.1 / IRN 37343). Searchable online catalog.

ushmm.org

North-China Daily News (Brill)

Full digital archive of Shanghai's most influential English newspaper (1864–1950, with wartime break). Potential source for retail advertisements of Shanghai Millionaire.

brilloneline.com

Key References

Books & Academic Works

  • Reed, Christopher A. Gutenberg in Shanghai: Chinese Print Capitalism, 1876–1937. Vancouver: UBC Press, 2004. — The definitive study of Shanghai's printing industry; essential for understanding the printer candidates.
  • Henchion, Paul. The Shanghai Millionaire. In: various collector publications. — Secondary literature on the game itself.
  • Barr, Betty. Personal recollections of Lunghua Civilian Assembly Centre. — Primary source on camp cultural life.
  • Ma, Changlin 马长林. 老上海行名辞典 1880–1941 (English-Chinese Hong List of Old Shanghai). Shanghai: Shanghai Ancient Books Publishing House, 2008. — The source of the Zenodo business directory dataset.

Institutional Resources

  • Shanghai Library (上海图书馆) — Xujiahui Bibliotheca Zi-Ka-Wei (徐家汇藏书楼): holds original Hong Lists and commercial registration archives
  • Shanghai Municipal Archives (上海市档案馆): business registration records for foreign companies in the International Settlement
  • CSUN University Library: Civilian Internment in China, 1941–1945 — statistical data on camp populations
  • Center for Jewish History (CJH): Shanghai Collection — everyday life of Jewish refugees, 1939–1948
  • Leo Baeck Institute: German-Jewish refugee periodicals from Shanghai

Online Collections

  • Historical Photographs of China (University of Bristol) — includes images of Shanghai printing works
  • Virtual Shanghai — maps, texts, statistical data on historical Shanghai
  • Industrial History of Hong Kong — detailed Kelly & Walsh company history
  • Geographicus Rare Antique Maps — Kelly & Walsh imprints and publication history

Research Methodology

Material Analysis

Physical examination of the commercial edition: board construction, printing technique, paper quality, label marks, color palette.

Archival Research

Cross-referencing business directories (Hong List), industrial surveys (SMP 1935–1940), and newspaper advertisements to narrow printer candidates.

Historical Mapping

Using Virtual Shanghai maps and GIS data to locate printing firms within the International Settlement and trace their spatial relationship to retail outlets.

Comparative Design

Side-by-side analysis with Waddington's London edition to identify production techniques, copying errors, and localization decisions.
